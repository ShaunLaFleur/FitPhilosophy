The Routine

Workout A:

  • Bench Press 5x5
  • Squat 5x5
  • Romanian Deadlift 3x10
  • Pendlay Row 3x8
  • Cable Tricep Pushdowns 3x12
  • Barbell Curls 3x12
  • Band or Cable Facepulls 3x12

Workout B:

  • Overhead Press 5x5
  • Floor Press 5x5
  • Deadlift 5x5 (4 warmup sets and 1 heavy work set)
  • Box Squat 5x5
  • Weighted Chins 3x8
  • Reverse Hyperextensions or Hyperextensions 3x15

Q&A Guide

Q: In what order do I perform these workouts and on which days?

A: These workouts are performed on 3 non-consecutive days a week and are rotated. For example, the first week you would workout Monday, Wednesday and Friday by performing Workout A (Monday), Workout B (Wednesday), Workout A (Friday), and then taking two days off for rest. You would then continue the next week with Workout B since the last workout performed was Workout A on that previous Friday. This means that some weeks you will perform Workout A twice and on others you will perform Workout B twice.

Q: Why are hyperextensions and face pulls in this routine? Shouldn't beginners avoid too many additional accessory lifts?

A: The lower back and shoulders are arguably the most common areas that weight lifters injure, so as a "prehab", I like to have beginners really strengthen their lower back and rear deltoids to prevent any injuries in the future when they are lifting much heavier loads.
